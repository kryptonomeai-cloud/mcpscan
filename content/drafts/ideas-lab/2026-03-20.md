## 4. AI/ML Frontier

### Top Idea: Implement NanoGPT Slowrun's Chain Distillation Ensemble Technique

**NanoGPT Slowrun** (101 pts on HN) achieved **10x data efficiency** — an ensemble of 1.8B parameter models (18B total) trained on 100M tokens matches performance that normally requires 1B tokens. The key innovation: **chain distillation ensembles**.

**How it works:**
1. Train model M1 on data D
2. Train model M2 from scratch, using M1 as a teacher (knowledge distillation)
3. Repeat: each model learns from the previous one
4. At inference, average logits from all models

**The counterintuitive finding:** Individual models *overfit* with more training epochs (loss goes from 3.295 to 3.310). But the ensemble *improves* (3.185 to 3.166). The models learn different things when pushed past their individual optimum.

**Your play:** This is directly applicable to your 5x 3090 setup. You can train ensembles of smaller models (1-3B params each) on limited domain data and achieve results that would normally require 10x more data. For fine-tuning-as-a-service, this means:
- Clients with small datasets (common for SMEs) get much better results
- You can charge premium for "data-efficient fine-tuning"
- Each GPU can train a different ensemble member simultaneously

**Revenue Potential:** "Data-efficient fine-tuning" as a differentiator — charge £3,000-7,000 per project where data is limited. Most local fine-tuning services can't offer this.

### Quick Win: Clone and Run the Slowrun Benchmark

`git clone https://github.com/qlabs-eng/slowrun` — reproduce the 10x result on your GPU cluster. Understand the chain distillation setup. This gives you a concrete demo for the "data-efficient fine-tuning" pitch.
