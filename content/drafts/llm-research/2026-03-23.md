## New Releases

### Mistral Small 4 (119B / 6.5B active) — Mar 17, 2026
- **Provider:** Mistral AI
- **Architecture:** MoE — 128 experts, 4 active per token
- **Context:** 256k tokens
- **Capabilities:** Multimodal (text+image input), configurable reasoning effort (`none` for fast, `high` for deep reasoning), function calling, JSON output
- **License:** Apache 2.0
- **Key wins:** Matches/surpasses GPT-OSS-120B on AA-LCR, LiveCodeBench, AIME while generating 20-75% shorter outputs. 40% latency reduction and 3x throughput vs Small 3.
- **Infra requirements:** Min 4× H100-80GB; recommended 4× H100 or 2× DGX B200
- **Significance:** First model to unify Magistral (reasoning), Pixtral (vision), and Devstral (coding) into one. Founding member of NVIDIA Nemotron Coalition.
- **Links:** [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/mistralai/Mistral-Small-4-119B-2603), [Blog](https://mistral.ai/news/mistral-small-4)

### Leanstral (119B / 6B active) — Mar 17, 2026
- **Provider:** Mistral AI
- **Purpose:** First open-source Lean 4 proof engineering agent
- **Architecture:** Same Mistral Small 4 base, fine-tuned for formal proofs
- **License:** Apache 2.0
- **Key results:** On FLTEval, Leanstral pass@16 ($290) scores 31.9 vs Claude Opus 4.6 at 39.6 ($1,650) — 92x cheaper for ~80% of Opus quality. Beats Sonnet 4.6 by 8 points.
- **Links:** [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/mistralai/Leanstral-2603), [Blog](https://mistral.ai/news/leanstral)

### Mistral Forge — Mar 17, 2026
- Enterprise system for building frontier-grade AI models grounded in proprietary knowledge. Details limited but signals Mistral's push into enterprise fine-tuning services.

### NVIDIA Nemotron Cascade 2 (30B / 3B active) — ~Mar 22, 2026
- **Provider:** NVIDIA
- **Architecture:** MoE, post-trained from Nemotron-3-Nano-30B-A3B-Base
- **Key achievements:**
  - 🏅 IMO 2025 gold medal (35 pts)
  - 🏅 IOI 2025 gold medal (439.3)
  - 🏅 ICPC World Finals 2025 (10/12 problems)
  - AIME 2025: 92.4 (98.6 with tools)
  - LiveCodeBench v6: 87.2
  - SWE Verified (OpenHands): 50.2
- **Significance:** Only 3B active params achieving olympiad-gold performance. Remarkable efficiency.
- **Links:** [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/nvidia/Nemotron-Cascade-2-30B-A3B), [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19220)

### NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Super (120B / 12B active) — Mar 11, 2026
- **Architecture:** LatentMoE — hybrid Mamba-2 + MoE + Attention, with Multi-Token Prediction (MTP)
- **Context:** Up to 1M tokens
- **Infra:** Min 8× H100-80GB; NVFP4 quantized version for single B200/DGX Spark
- **Key benchmarks:** AIME25 90.2, SWE-Bench Verified 60.5 (OpenHands), RULER@1M 91.75
- **License:** NVIDIA Nemotron Open Model License (commercial OK)
- **Links:** [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/nvidia/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Super-120B-A12B-BF16)

### MiniMax M2.5 (229B) — ~Mar 10, 2026
- **Provider:** MiniMaxAI (Chinese lab)
- **Key claims:**
  - SWE-Bench Verified: 80.2% (SOTA claim)
  - Multi-SWE-Bench: 51.3%
  - BrowseComp: 76.3%
  - 37% faster than M2.1, matching Claude Opus 4.6 speed
- **Pricing:** "$1/hour at 100 tok/s" — positions as "intelligence too cheap to meter"
- **Strengths:** Coding across 10+ languages, agentic search, office work (Excel/Word/PPT), full-stack development
- **Links:** [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/MiniMaxAI/MiniMax-M2.5)

### Rakuten AI 3.0 (671B / 37B active) — Mar 17, 2026

- **Context:** 128k tokens
- **License:** Apache 2.0

## Benchmarks & Comparisons

### The "3B Active" Sweet Spot
Both Nemotron Cascade 2 (3B active) and Qwen3.5-35B-A3B (3B active) are proving that MoE models with very small active parameter counts can compete with much larger dense models:
- Nemotron Cascade 2: IMO gold with 3B active
- Qwen3.5-35B-A3B: 2.4M downloads, matching models 10x its active size

## Relevance to Us (Mac mini + 5× RTX 3090 GPU Server)

### Worth Testing Now
1. **Qwen3.5-35B-A3B (GGUF)** — Only 3B active params, total 35B. Should run well on our 3090 setup via llama.cpp. The 2M+ downloads of unsloth GGUFs suggest strong community validation. Multimodal + reasoning.

2. **Nemotron Cascade 2 (30B/3B active)** — Similarly tiny active footprint. Exceptional math/code reasoning. Worth testing for coding tasks.

3. **Qwen3.5-9B** — Small enough for single 3090, hybrid Delta+Attention architecture, 262k context. Good daily-driver candidate.

### Watch List
4. **Mistral Small 4 NVFP4** — At 119B total but 6.5B active, the NVFP4 quantization might squeeze onto our setup. Needs testing.

5. **MiniMax M2.5** — 229B total is too large for local, but the API pricing ("$1/hour") makes it worth considering as a hosted option for coding tasks.

### Not Practical for Our Hardware
- Nemotron 3 Super (needs 8× H100)
- Rakuten AI 3.0 (671B total, needs 8× H100)

### Key Takeaway
The MoE efficiency revolution continues. Models with 3-6B active parameters are now matching or beating much larger dense models. Our 5× RTX 3090 setup (~120GB VRAM total) is well-positioned for the 30-35B MoE class.
