## New Releases

### Mistral Small 4 (119B-2603)
- **Date:** ~March 23, 2026
- **Architecture:** MoE — 128 experts, 4 active per token, 119B total / 6.5B active
- **Context:** 256k tokens
- **Key features:**
  - Unifies Instruct, Reasoning (Magistral), and Devstral capabilities in one model
  - Multimodal (text + image input)
  - Configurable reasoning effort (none/high) per request
  - 40% lower latency, 3x more throughput vs Mistral Small 3
  - Trained eagle head for speculative decoding
  - NVFP4 quantization checkpoint available
- **License:** Apache 2.0
- **Benchmarks:** Competitive with GPT-OSS 120B on AIME, LiveCodeBench, AA-LCR with significantly shorter outputs (1.6K vs 5.8-6.1K chars)
- **Source:** https://huggingface.co/mistralai/Mistral-Small-4-119B-2603

### RYS II — Repeated Layers Research
- Fascinating new research: duplicating middle transformer layers in Qwen3.5-27B yields quality improvements with no training
- Released models: RYS-Qwen3.5-27B-FP8 in S/M/L/XL variants
- Finding: LLMs appear to "think in a universal language" — middle layer representations are more similar cross-language (same content) than same-language (different content)
- Source: https://dnhkng.github.io/posts/rys-ii/

### Delta-KV Compression for llama.cpp
- New technique applying video codec concepts (keyframe + delta) to KV cache compression
- Results on Llama 3.1 70B: near-lossless 4-bit KV (perplexity 3.3352 vs F16 baseline 3.3389)
- Standard Q4_0 was 3.5385 (~6% worse)
- Bonus: 10% faster decode with weight-skip optimization
- No training required, directly integrated into llama.cpp fork
- Source: https://github.com/cenconq25/delta-compress-llm

## Relevance to Us (Mac mini + 5x RTX 3090 GPU server)

### 🟢 High Relevance

1. **Nemotron-Cascade-2-30B-A3B** — Only 3B active params, should run extremely well on a single RTX 3090 with GGUF quantization. IMO/IOI gold medal math reasoning in a model that fits on consumer hardware. This is the most exciting release for our setup.

2. **Delta-KV compression for llama.cpp** — Direct performance improvement for any model we're already running. Near-lossless 4-bit KV cache = longer contexts without quality loss. Worth trying immediately.

3. **RYS-Qwen3.5-27B** — The XL variant might push VRAM limits but S/M should fit. Free quality improvement via layer duplication. Watch for GGUF releases.

4. **Kimi K2.5** — If Cursor considers it best-in-class for coding, worth evaluating as our local coding model.

### 🟡 Medium Relevance

5. **Mistral Small 4 (119B)** — 6.5B active params is great, but 119B total is large for GGUF. Lower quantizations (Q3/Q4) might fit across 5 GPUs. The NVFP4 checkpoint is interesting but needs NVIDIA's stack. Monitor for community GGUFs.

6. **Nemotron-3-Nano-4B** — GGUF already available. Very small but could serve as a fast draft model for speculative decoding.

### 🔴 Out of Reach

7. **Nemotron-3-Super-120B-A12B** — Requires 8× H100-80GB minimum. Not practical for our setup.
8. **RakutenAI-3.0 (671B)** — Way too large.
