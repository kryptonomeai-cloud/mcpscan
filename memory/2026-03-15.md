4. `rsi > 22` → easy to pass, not the blocker

The strategy description literally says it was backtested in a -49.35% bear market. BTC/ETH/SOL are likely trading well BELOW their EMA150 right now. This single condition probably blocks 100% of entry signals.

**Secondary issue: ROI targets are astronomical for paper trading observation.**
- `minimal_roi = {'0': 0.491}` → needs 49.1% profit to close via ROI at entry! For a paper trading test where you just want to see if it trades, this means even if a trade opens, it won't close for a very long time.

**WebSocket errors:** Non-critical. Connection drops with code 1006 (server-side close) periodically; falls back to REST API cleanly. Doesn't prevent analysis.

**Verbosity:** Set to 0 (minimum). No signal-level debug output — can't tell which condition is failing per candle without bumping to `-vv`.

### Recommendations
1. **Most likely fix:** The strategy is backtested on data where entries were viable. In current bear market conditions, `close > ema150` is probably never true on BTC/ETH/SOL. Consider temporarily removing that filter or switching to a different strategy to test the pipeline.
2. **Add verbosity** (`-vv` or `-v`) to see per-candle signal output and confirm exactly which condition is blocking.

## ~00:45 — Freqtrade Deep Dive (Subagent Follow-up)

### Confirmed: Strategy is structurally sound but too selective for live market

**Full strategy code reviewed** (`EMACrossOptimised.py`). Entry requires ALL 5 conditions simultaneously:

| # | Condition | Frequency | Blocker? |
|---|-----------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | EMA7 crosses above EMA44 (exact crossover candle) | ~1x per 2-4 weeks per pair | ⚠️ Rare |
| 2 | Close > EMA150 (bullish trend filter) | Depends on market regime | 🔴 **Likely blocking ALL signals in current bear market** |
| 3 | Volume > 1.46x 20-period average | ~20-30% of candles | ⚠️ Eliminates many crossovers |
| 4 | RSI > 22 | ~95%+ of candles | ✅ Not a blocker |
| 5 | Volume > 0 | Always true | ✅ Trivial |

**With only 3 pairs on 1h = 72 candles/day.** An EMA 7/44 crossover happens maybe once every 2-4 weeks per pair. Add the volume spike requirement AND the trend filter, and you could go **months** without a single entry signal.

### Why zero trades in 27+ hours is expected (not a bug)

1. **The strategy was hyperopt-optimised on 6 months of mixed data** — it found parameters that generated only 17 trades in 6 months (per its docstring: "94% win rate (16/17)"). That's roughly 1 trade every 10 days.
2. **27 hours without a trade is completely normal for this strategy.** You'd need weeks to see even one signal.
3. **Bear market amplifies the drought** — if BTC/ETH/SOL are below their 150-period hourly EMA, condition #2 kills every signal.

### WebSocket Issues (non-critical but notable)
- Binance WS connections dropping with code 1006 (server-side close) at ~22:09 UTC
- Bot falls back to REST API for candle data — confirmed in logs: "Couldn't reuse watch for BTC/USDT, 1h, falling back to REST api"
- WS reset again at 00:02 UTC with "Connector is closed" errors
- **Not causing missed trades** — REST fallback works correctly

### Recommendations
1. **If you want to see trades within days, not weeks:**
   - Remove or relax `close > ema150` (the trend filter)
   - Lower the volume multiplier from 1.46 to 1.0 or 1.1
   - Or switch to a different strategy (MomentumTrendStrategy, EMACrossBull, etc.)
2. **If you want to validate the strategy is working at all:**
   - Add `-vv` flag to see per-candle signal evaluations
   - Or run a backtest on recent data: `docker exec freqtrade freqtrade backtesting --strategy EMACrossOptimised --timerange 20260101-`
3. **If you want to keep the strategy but see more action:**
   - Add more pairs (top 20 by volume) — more pairs = more crossover opportunities
   - Consider 15m or 5m timeframe variants

### Verdict
**Not a bug. The strategy is working correctly — it's just very conservative by design.** 17 trades in 6 months means ~1 trade per 10 days. Zero trades in 27 hours is statistically expected. The bot is alive, processing candles hourly, but the entry conditions are almost never met in the current market.
